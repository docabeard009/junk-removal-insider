Whether it's decades of accumulated tools, old furniture, or just years of "I'll deal with that later" — here's how to finally clear your NJ garage for good.

A professional junk removal crew can clear most standard garages in 1–3 hours, hauling everything away so you don't have to make multiple dump runs or rent a dumpster and load it yourself. How to Prepare for Your Garage Cleanout

A little preparation before the crew arrives makes the job faster — which can mean a lower final price if you're paying by volume.

Before the crew arrives:

  • Pull out anything you're keeping and move it to the driveway or inside the house
  • Remove hazardous materials yourself — gas cans, oil-based paint, pesticides can't go in the truck
  • Drain fuel from mowers and power equipment before removal
  • Disconnect and empty any mini-fridges or freezers 24 hours ahead
  • Have a clear path from the garage to the street for the crew to load efficiently

What Haulers Will and Won't Take From a Garage

Standard items: Old furniture stored in garage, broken appliances, bikes and sporting equipment, cardboard boxes and packing materials, tools and hardware, shelving units, tires (usually a small upcharge per tire), yard equipment.

Special handling required: Gas cans with fuel, oil-based paint, propane tanks, automotive fluids, fluorescent shop lights. These are regulated under NJ DEP rules and cannot go in a standard junk truck. Warren County hosts periodic HHW collection events for these materials.
