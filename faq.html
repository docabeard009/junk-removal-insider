What factors affect the price of junk removal?

The main drivers are: volume (how much of the truck you fill), weight (heavy materials like concrete cost more to dispose), labor difficulty (stairs, narrow hallways, tight spaces), material type (electronics and appliances often have disposal fees), distance from the disposal facility, and timing (same-day service may carry a small premium).

Is junk removal worth it compared to renting a dumpster?

For most NJ homeowners, professional junk removal is more cost-effective than dumpster rental once you factor in the dumpster drop-off fee ($150–$250), the weekly rental period, your own labor loading it, and the final pickup and disposal fee. A junk removal crew does all the loading — you don't lift a thing. The math typically favors hiring a crew for anything under 3 full truckloads.

Do I need to be home for junk removal?

For interior jobs, yes — someone needs to let the crew in and indicate what's going. For exterior work (yard debris, items staged outside), many operators can work without you present with prior arrangement.

What Gets Removed

What will junk removal companies take in NJ?

Most household items: furniture of all types, mattresses and box springs, appliances (washers, dryers, fridges, stoves, dishwashers), electronics, clothing, boxes and general clutter, yard waste, light construction debris, bikes and sporting equipment, shelving, and more.

What items CAN'T go in a junk removal truck in NJ?

Items prohibited by NJ DEP regulations include: oil-based paint and solvents, propane tanks with fuel, fluorescent bulbs and ballasts, car and lead-acid batteries, pesticides, herbicides, motor oil, and other hazardous materials. These require disposal through Warren County's HHW program or certified hazmat facilities.

Do junk removal companies take electronics?

Yes, most will — but many charge a small surcharge per item for TVs, monitors, and computers because NJ requires electronics to be processed through certified e-waste recyclers rather than landfilled. Ask upfront about electronics fees.

How It Works

How does junk removal work, step by step?

1) Call or book online for a free on-site estimate. 2) The crew arrives, assesses your load, and gives you a firm price. 3) You approve — or don't, no obligation. 4) They do all the heavy lifting, loading, and hauling. 5) Payment is due at completion. 6) Materials are sorted for donation, recycling, and disposal at licensed facilities.

How quickly can I get junk removed in NJ?

Most local operators including Hackettstown Junk Removal offer same-day or next-day service. Spring (March–May) is peak season and books out faster — schedule at least a few days ahead if possible.

What happens to my stuff after it's removed?

Responsible haulers follow a disposal hierarchy: usable items go to donation partners (Habitat for Humanity ReStore, local thrift networks), metals and electronics go to specialized recyclers, and what remains goes to licensed NJ transfer stations. Up to 60–70% of a typical residential load can be diverted from landfill.

What questions should I ask before hiring a junk removal company?

Are you licensed and insured in NJ? Do you provide free on-site estimates? Do you have a donation policy for usable items? Are there any items you won't take? Is your pricing by volume or flat rate? Are disposal fees included in the quote?

How do I avoid getting scammed by a junk removal company?

Red flags include: companies that only give phone quotes without seeing your items, upfront payment required before any work, no physical address or online presence, no proof of insurance, unusually low quotes that grow significantly on arrival, and no clear answer about where your materials go. Stick with established local operators with verifiable reviews.
