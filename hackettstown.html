What Hackettstown Haulers Will and Won't Take

Most household items can be removed in a standard junk removal job. This includes furniture of all sizes, mattresses, box springs, appliances (washers, dryers, refrigerators, dishwashers), electronics, yard waste, bagged garbage, clothing, toys, general clutter, and light construction debris.

Items that require special handling under NJ DEP regulations and cannot go in a standard junk truck include: oil-based paint and solvents, propane tanks with fuel, fluorescent bulbs and ballasts, car and lead-acid batteries, pesticides and herbicides, and certain electronics depending on condition. Warren County hosts periodic Household Hazardous Waste collection events for these materials.

Hackettstown-Specific Considerations

Hackettstown's older residential neighborhoods — particularly those closer to Main Street and the historic district — can present access challenges for larger hauling vehicles. Narrow streets, limited driveway space, and tight turns are common. Local operators who know the borough well are better equipped to deploy appropriately-sized vehicles than national dispatch services routing crews from distant locations.

The borough also has specific bulk waste pickup rules for residents who prefer curbside removal for smaller loads — check with the Hackettstown Department of Public Works for current schedules. For anything beyond a few items, a professional hauler is almost always faster and more cost-effective than waiting for a bulk pickup date.
