Furniture Removal in New Jersey: Free, Cheap & Fast Options

How NJ homeowners can get rid of old sofas, beds, tables, and more — whether it's worth donating, selling, or just calling a hauler.

Getting rid of old furniture in New Jersey comes down to one core question: is the piece in good enough shape to donate or sell, or does it need to just go? That answer determines whether you're looking at a free pickup or a paid hauler — and knowing which route makes sense saves you time, money, and the hassle of a failed Facebook Marketplace listing.

Free vs. Paid: Which Route is Right For Your Furniture?

✓ Free Options

Donation Pickup

Habitat for Humanity ReStore and similar organizations will pick up furniture free if it's in good, usable condition. Call ahead — they're selective and may not take everything.

✓ Free Options

Facebook Marketplace / Craigslist

List it as "free, must haul" and most decent pieces will be claimed within 24–48 hours. Works well for solid wood pieces, vintage items, and functional furniture.

$ Paid Options

Junk Removal Crew

Fastest and most reliable for worn, damaged, or large-quantity furniture. They do all the carrying — no waiting for buyers who don't show. Best for sofas, mattresses, and anything in poor condition.

$ Paid Options

Municipal Bulk Pickup

Free but slow — requires scheduling and following strict borough rules about what can be set out and when. Good for 1–2 items if you have time.

What Furniture Haulers Won't Take

Standard furniture — sofas, chairs, tables, dressers, beds — is accepted by all NJ junk removal companies. Items that may be refused or incur upcharges: furniture with confirmed bed bug infestation (a major liability issue), extremely heavy items like solid marble tables or cast-iron pieces, and furniture in storage units that require access fees.
